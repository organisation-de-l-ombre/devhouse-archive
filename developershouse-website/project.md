IMR (International Media Referencing) is a complete and
online cinematographic database. It references a wide
choice of movies and series, and it  was updated
continously. In addition, it is a completely opened
database which allows easilly any user to publish
cinematographic content and to improve the present content.
The website of IMR has a modern, an ergonomic and a user
friendly user interface, to offer you the best navigation
experience as possible! Take a coffee and have a look
[here](https://imr.media), hoping it pleases you 😎

## Some words about the project
IMR taken months and months to be developed and released.
The platform has been designed by young and ambitious
developers who love web development, cinematography and
of course to work together ❤️ But the most important is
our developers, our community, and people who help us and
who contribute to the project, to assure it a better future!
Thus, we thank a lot our developers and our community, without
them IMR could not have been realized!

## Why trust us?
First, a great people number already trust us, so why not you?
Next, IMR is intended to be an alternative to other online
cinematographic databases such as [IMDB](https://imdb.com) or
[The Movie Database](https://themoviedb.org). Alternatives are
very important in the World Wide Web and allow users to use a
similar services but which differ anyway according to their
approach, policy or to their way to attract users, etc...

And finally, such as every service, every platform, IMR has its
own advantages compared to alternatives:

* Complete and flexible
* Innovative and intuitive user interface
* Completely opened database (easy for data management and
  contributions)
* Developed with reliable and powerful technologies
* Entirely responsive interface for mobile compatibility

We know that IMR is a new platform, and certainly lacking of
experience, but it is by trusting projects which emerge
that the grow up!
