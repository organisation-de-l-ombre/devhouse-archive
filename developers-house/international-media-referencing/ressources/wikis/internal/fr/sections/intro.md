## What is IMR?
IMR (International Media referencing) is a complete and online database which
references a great deal of cinematographic content (movies, series, etc...).
Although services such as [IMDB](https://imdb.com) or
[The Movie Database](https://themoviedb.org) already exist, IMR wishes to propose
an alternative, more complete and more flexible than other online cinematographic
databases. The goal is to reference the most possible information in only one
source, to make the navigation easier and more intuitive for users. The second IMR
goal is to reference/update the content openly as on
[Wikipédia](https://wikipedia.org) or [Fandom](https://fandom.com). Thus, users can
contribute to the project and can improve content quality.

## How the project came about?
As the initial project creator and developer is a huge fan of cinematographic
content, this ingenious idea crossed over his mind. At the beginning, this
project started from nothing and the only goal was to learn web development,
more precisely fullstack web development (UI/Interfaces + API/Data managing).
Finally, after months of work the project realized and was born.

## Advantages and drawbacks
Here are listed most of the advantages and the drawbacks of the platform. We hope
it can help you to in your personal opinion compared to other online databases.

### Advantages
* Complete and flexible
* Innovative and intuitive user interface
* Completely opened database (easy for data management and contributions)
* Developed with reliable and powerful technologies
* Entirely responsive interface for mobile compatibility

### Drawbacks
* New platform (lack of experience)
* Functionalities implemented progressively (to propose new features regularly and to power
  in production stable and reliable platform)
* Developed and maintained by young developers, not by professionals (but is it really a
  problem 🤔)
* Not as many references compared to major online databases
  ([IMDB](https://imdb.com) or [The Movie Database](https://themoviedb.org))
